Introduction #1

"The Basics" - Education, Career, Interests

Welcome to my personal website!

I am an experienced Math Tutor. I am also a Florida Tech alumnus in Applied Mathematics, with an Applied Area of Computer Science!

Actually, let me start from the beginning:

I was Homeschooled, and I started at Indian River State College (IRSC) at 14 years old. I had a perfect GPA most of my time there, and I started tutoring Calculus (among other maths) there when I was 17. I worked in the Math Lab primarily, but also in classrooms and the Academic Support Center (ASC). I became the president of Delta Mu Epsilon (Math Club). Upon graduating high school, I received my AA in Mathematics from IRSC, with a 3.97 GPA! Because of these things (and my SAT superscore of 1500/1600), I was placed on the 2019 All-Florida Academic Team (which is the top 0.02% of students enrolled in 2 year colleges in Florida).

I was very blessed to have received merit-based, full-ride offers (100% of tuition paid) from multiple universities, including the Florida Institute of Technology, which I decided to attend. During my time there, I was involved in FITSEC (Cybersecurity Club), CPFIT (Competitive Programming Club), and lightly involved in AMS (American Mathematical Society). In December 2023, I received my BS in Applied Mathematics (Applied Area: Computer Science) from FIT, with a 3.68 GPA!

The Summer before I graduated, I was chosen for a competitive internship at none other than Royal Caribbean Group! The internship's focus was using Data Analysis on Cybersecurity trends within the organization. I gained valuable hands-on experience in Power BI, Excel, and Python.

Since completing my BS at FIT, I have returned to be a Math Tutor again at IRSC! I love IRSC so much, I came back to work here a 2nd time, haha! I absolutely love immersing myself in an environment of learning and growth. I also am quite lucky to have such kind colleagues/bosses, in a place where we all have each other's backs. I often hang out there after I work; it is that much of an enjoyable place to be, and I'm happy to be celebrating one year here (again)!

Outside of work, I enjoy spending time with others, doing my hobbies, self-studying, and overall trying to live my best life! I always have a lot going on with my aquatic animals - it's like a fish hospital in my room sometimes, except I'm the only "nurse" so it's constantly short-staffed... going for the realistic look I guess! But, trying to keep my head above water. It fills me with hope when I see new "eggnant" mama shrimp, new fish eggs in the tank, sometimes new baby snails - even just a shrimp molting successfully is a great sign! And of course, my dogs are extremely clingy, snoutsy, constantly wanting attention and walks - I love saying "yes" to those precious faces! It's so wonderful to come home and feel like a celebrity hounded by the pup-erazzi. Throughout the day, they'll even follow me room-to-room, to be up-close and snuggly! They know who loves them (hint: it's me!). I am also extremely lucky to have a best friend so close to me, who I've been friends with since before 2010! She is the kindest person I know and she is always my inspiration of what a "good" person looks like. Finally, before bed, I track my habits of the day in my spreadsheet. I like having an accountability buddy to help analyze what went wrong during the day and how to fix it. I always want to be improving and being the best version of myself!

  10. "Set your mind on things above..." ~ Colossians 3:2

Note: that last one, commonly known as "The Golden Rule," not only "summarizes the law and prophets," but also summarizes my own philosophy of life. As someone with Christian roots, I remember the story of the servant who owed his master a great debt. The master chose to have mercy and forgive the servant's debt. However, the servant did not extend that same mercy; instead, he threatened a 2nd servant to repay to himself a much smaller debt.

Since God granted me a life free of crippling physical pain, a life free from imprisonment, a life of seeing the sunlight, I want to extend these same basic mercies to animals. That is why I want to become mostly Vegan: I have a duty to extend the same mercies that were shown to me. I could have been born a cow just as easily as I could have been born a human, after all. It is a matter of love and deep respect for life.

Finally, on a slightly different note, I try to remember that other people, even the ones who are full of hate and anger, are also living beings, capable of both love and pain. I'd rather the most evil people alive seek rehabilitation and self-improvement, rather than suffering and death. I don't think anyone is beyond redemption at any point in their lives. I've made mistakes in the past too, and I want to continually work to become the best version of myself that I can be. To steal some physics terms: what matters more in life than your position, is your velocity.
